Toxic level of our product

Plastics:

The team of researchers, led by Stefanie Hellweg, a professor of ecological systems design at the Swiss university ETH Zurich, identified a whopping 10,500 chemicals in the plastics they studied.

Of these chemicals, about a quarter—2,480—were “substances of potential concern,” which means there is some evidence to indicate they are harmful. In some cases, the chemicals are known to be toxic to aquatic life, cancer-causing, or damaging to specific organs. In others, the chemicals can accumulate in humans and animals, causing problems such as memory loss over time.
Helene Wiesinger, lead author of the study and a doctoral student at ETH Zurich, says that at least 1,000 of the chemicals identified as substances of concern can be harmful even in small doses. She also says that if something is a carcinogen there is no safe level of exposure. She tells , “Even at very low levels, these can lead to cancer, and obviously gets worse with high doses. With endocrine disruptors, small doses can be problematic. With these chemicals, using any at all is a problem.”

Newspapers:

Lead, mercury and cadmium were found to be below their measurement detection limits (4ppm, 5ppm and 1ppm respectively) in nearly all samples. Barium and total chromium levels were highest in supplements printed on “glossy” paper. Copper was present at levels around 20‐30ppm in the standard newsprint sections and at levels of 80ppm or more in the supplements. Copper concentrations increased by 33‐40 percent which correlated well with the increased colour content. A scientific appraisal and review showed that the heavy metal contents of newspapers are highly unlikely to pose any environmental risk or to have any health implications.

Electronic waste::

Burning e-waste can generate dioxins, furans, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), polyhalogenated aromatic hydrocarbons (PHAHs) and hydrogen chloride, some of which are carcinogenic, all of which are toxic. Animals, plants, and communities – even those miles away from a recycling site – may be exposed and cause harm.Some of the e-waste are radio active.

How this project keep environment safe?

Our project is about recycling the waste materials specially paper, plastic and electronics materials. These waste are very harmful for our environment. If we can be successful in this project, these types of harmful wastes can be reduced. Through our project we can reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills and incinerators. We can conserve natural resources such as timber, water and minerals.

It will increases economic security by tapping a domestic source of materials. It will prevents pollution by reducing the need to collect new raw materials. It will save energy as the materials will be recycled and reused gradually. It will the prevention of greenhouse gases (GHGs) and supporting local economies. This recycling program will also help to improve water and air quality and build blocks for sustainable growing communities. Thus our project will help to keep the environment safe.
